The begining

at the very begining you have the harvard mach 1 also known as ASCC. The harvard mach 1 concept was brought to IBM by a man named Howard Aiken in 1937. this was then built and sent to harvard in 1944. this machine was huge it had hundreds of miles of wire and had over 700,000 electromechanical parts and mostly everything was mechanical. this device was used for math like a calculator. it could do three addition or 3 subtractions problems in 1 second, mulyiplication in 6,division in 15.3 and complex math equations or functions in over a minute. This was revolutionary for the time.

the second step

the next big developement happened in 1948 with invention of the transistor replacing vacuum tubes. this was joint invention by three men named William Shockley, John Bardeen and Walter Brattain. The transistor is a device used to boost electronic signals. This device is in all sorts of electronics from radios to computers. this invention dramatically reduces the size of the devices, especially computers.

third step

the next large developement was the integrated circuit. This also helped cut down the size dramatically.the first patent was givin to a man named robert noyce but there was also a patent pending by jack kilby soon after they were both acknowledged as having created it at seperately.in the simplist term what they do is control how much electronic current flows through.

fourth step

the next big leap was the microprocessor.A microprocessor is basically a integrated chip but more compicated. A microprocessor mainly consists of three parts a control unit, an ALU and a register array. these things pretty much tell the computer what to do and when to do it. It was created by four men either architect or engineer named Federico Faggin, Marcian (Ted) Hoff, Stanley Mazor , and Masatoshi Shima. All of them were working for a japanese calculator company

fifth step

in the fifth generation of computers it really steps up with the invention of many different things such as artificial inteligence and VSLI these things put together is your fifth gen computer. VLSI is basically where it would would take a bunch of other signals and transmit them to the computer. This is another invention by jack kilby which he created while working for TI.
